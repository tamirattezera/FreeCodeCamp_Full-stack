Alan Turing

The father of modern computer science

Alan Turing, mathematician, computer scientist, and pioneer of artificial intelligence.

Biography

Alan Turing was a British mathematician and computer scientist whose ideas became the foundation of modern computing. During World War II, he contributed to breaking encrypted messages and helped develop early computing machines.

Major Contributions

  • Created the concept of the Turing Machine.
  • Made foundational contributions to artificial intelligence.
  • Helped decode encrypted communications during World War II.
  • Introduced the famous Turing Test for machine intelligence.

Timeline

1912

Alan Turing was born in London, England.

1936

Published his paper introducing the Turing Machine.

1940s

Worked on codebreaking during World War II.

Legacy

Alan Turing's ideas continue to influence computer science, artificial intelligence, cryptography, and software engineering.
